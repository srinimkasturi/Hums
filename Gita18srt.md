## How to Use This Instrument

When a significant action, decision, or reaction occurs, invoke each layer in sequence:

1. **L0 — Pre-Check**: Is honest self-assessment possible?
1. **L1 — Ignition**: Name the action precisely before any diagnosis
1. **L2–L7 — Component Diagnosis**: Diagnose each of the six components against the four Guna states
1. **L8 — Mismatch Read**: Where are the Guna states inconsistent? The mismatch *is* the insight
1. **L9 — Binding Test**: Is the pattern creating karmic binding?
1. **L10 — Svadharma Calibration**: Is the diagnosis calibrated to this person’s natural role?
1. **L11 — Exit Check**: Is the instrument still needed, or has the seeing done its work?

> **The instrument does not score. It locates and reveals.**

## L8 · Mismatch Read

**Layer Role:** Where are the Guna states inconsistent across components?
**Element:** E13 · Cross-Component Pattern

After completing L2–L7, map each component to its Guna state:

|Component  |Guna State Identified|
|-----------|---------------------|
|L2 · Jñāna |                     |
|L3 · Karma |                     |
|L4 · Kartā |                     |
|L5 · Buddhi|                     |
|L6 · Dhṛti |                     |
|L7 · Sukham|                     |


> **The mismatch IS the insight.** A uniform row is rare and probably dishonest. The gaps show exactly where the binding lives. A Sattvic Buddhi with a Tamasic Dhṛti is the person who knows what is right but cannot hold to it. A Rajasic Kartā with a Sattvic Karma is the person doing the right thing for the wrong reason. Name the pattern precisely. *(18.19)*

## L9 · Binding Test

**Layer Role:** Is this component creating karmic binding?
**Element:** E14 · Binding Check

|State         |Description                                                                                                                   |Verse|
|--------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----|
|**Bound**     |Craving for result, aversion to consequence, or ego-claiming of outcome is present. Active binding. This is where the work is.|18.17|
|**Unbound**   |The Guna state is present but not tightening. The pattern exists without gripping. Awareness is sufficient here.              |18.17|
|**Dissolving**|The binding that was present is loosening through witnessing. No intervention needed beyond continued awareness.              |18.17|

## L10 · Svadharma Calibration

**Layer Role:** Is this diagnosis calibrated to the person’s natural role and constitution?
**Element:** E15 · Role Alignment

|State         |Description                                                                                                                                                                  |Verse   |
|--------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------|
|**Aligned**   |The action and its Guna expression fit the person’s natural constitution. Rajasic Karma in a leader-builder role, for instance, may be appropriate Svadharma — not deviation.|18.41–45|
|**Misaligned**|The action is against the person’s natural constitution — either performing another’s dharma or abandoning their own. Even well-done this creates friction.                  |18.47   |
|**Unclear**   |The person’s Svabhāva and Svadharma are not yet established. The instrument cannot calibrate. Clarify natural constitution first.                                            |18.45   |


> *The matrix is not a universal scale. It is role-sensitive. Diagnose against the person’s own nature — not against an abstract ideal.* *(18.41)*
